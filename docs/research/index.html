Research

Vessel Detection. AI detects three times more ocean vessels from satellite imagery, including smaller ships and coastal activity missed by public tracking systems.

Emissions. Industrial vessels emitted 1.3 billion tons of CO₂ in 2023, a 20% rise since 2016, mapped at global scale for the first time.

Transparency. Satellite mapping reveals 72–76% of industrial fishing vessels are not publicly tracked.

Open Data. The Open Ocean Project is the first global map of industrial human activity at sea, using AI and satellites to expose illegal fishing worldwide.

Dark Vessels. A neural network trained on SAR imagery detects vessels that deliberately disable their tracking transponders.

Vessel Tracking. Billions of vessel positions from satellite AIS reveal global traffic patterns and hotspots.

AI Challenge. xView3 produced the largest publicly labeled SAR dataset for maritime object detection, drawing over 800 teams from 64 countries.

Ice Loss. NASA laser altimetry reveals accelerating ice loss from Antarctica and Greenland, raising sea level by 14 mm over sixteen years.

Laser Altimetry. ICESat-2 fires six green laser beams to map ice, forests, and oceans with centimeter precision from 500 km in space.

Ice Velocity. ITS_LIVE tracks the speed and elevation of every glacier on Earth in near-real time, fusing satellite data spanning three decades.

Ocean Modeling. ECCO combines ocean models with satellite observations to reconstruct the complete three-dimensional circulation of the global ocean.

Ice Dynamics. Ice shelf thinning drives instantaneous ice sheet mass loss by reducing buttressing across a 150 km sector at the grounding line.

Ice Shelves. A 26-year satellite record shows West Antarctic ice shelf thinning has decelerated since the early 2000s, pointing to natural variability in ocean heat delivery.

Climate. El Niño–Southern Oscillation is the dominant interannual control on ice shelf mass balance in the Pacific sector of Antarctica.

Ice Shelves. Antarctic ice shelf volume loss is accelerating, with Western shelves losing mass 70% faster over the past decade.

Open Source. CAPToolkit is an open-source Python toolkit with over 60 tools for processing and integrating multi-satellite altimetry data.

Geodesy. Least-squares collocation combines satellite altimetry and shipborne gravimetry for an integrated marine gravity field.
