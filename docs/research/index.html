Research

An AI system detects three times more ocean vessels from satellite imagery, revealing smaller ships and coastal activity invisible to existing tracking systems.

Industrial vessels emitted 1.3 billion tons of CO₂ in 2023, a 20% rise since 2016, mapped for the first time at global scale.

Satellite mapping reveals 72–76% of industrial fishing vessels are not publicly tracked.

The Open Ocean Project is the first global map of industrial human activity at sea, using AI and satellites to expose illegal fishing worldwide.

A convolutional neural network trained on SAR satellite imagery detects vessels that deliberately disable their tracking transponders.

Billions of vessel positions from satellite AIS reveal global traffic patterns and hotspots.

xView3 produced the largest publicly labeled SAR dataset for maritime object detection, drawing over 800 teams from 64 countries.

Sixteen years of NASA laser altimetry reveal accelerating ice loss from Antarctica and Greenland.

ICESat-2 fires six green laser beams to map ice, forests, and oceans with centimeter precision from 500 km in space.

ITS_LIVE tracks the speed and elevation of every glacier and ice sheet on Earth in near-real time, fusing data from a fleet of satellites spanning three decades.

ECCO combines ocean models with satellite and in-situ observations to reconstruct the complete three-dimensional circulation of the global ocean.

Ice shelf thinning drives instantaneous ice sheet mass loss by reducing buttressing at the grounding line.

A 26-year satellite record shows West Antarctic ice shelf thinning has decelerated since the early 2000s, pointing to natural variability in ocean heat delivery.

El Niño–Southern Oscillation is the dominant interannual control on ice shelf mass balance in the Pacific sector of Antarctica.

Antarctic ice shelf volume loss is accelerating, with Western shelves losing mass 70% faster over the past decade.

CAPToolkit: an open-source Python toolkit for processing and integrating multi-satellite altimetry data.

Least-squares collocation combines satellite altimetry and shipborne gravimetry for an integrated marine gravity field.
